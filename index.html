CONNECT WITH NATURE

Organic farming is an old-age agricultural practice or technique that aims to use chemical-free products to maintain the overall integrity of the crops. It is also known as biological farming or ecological farming. Most contemporary agricultural practices use chemically induced fertilizers and pesticides for crop cultivation.
While this may have certain benefits, the use of chemical pesticides also has specific drawbacks. Organic farming, on the other hand, mostly deals with organic compost, green manure, or bone meal for improving crop yield. Other main traits of organic farming include a strong emphasis on companion planting and crop rotation.

ABOUT US

Business Inspiration and Model

Sarthi Organic Farm is a rural-based organic farming initiative located in the Chhota Udepur district, inspired by the principles of Subhash Palekar Natural Farming (SPNF). Subhash Palekar's model promotes sustainable and natural agricultural practices, known as "Zero Budget Natural Farming" (ZBNF), aiming to make farming financially viable without reliance on chemical inputs.

Mission and Vision

Mission: To provide fresh, high-quality, and 100% organic products directly to consumers, promoting a healthier lifestyle and an environmentally sustainable agriculture model.
Vision: To create a self-sustaining organic farm that becomes a model for other rural areas, contributing to healthier communities and a chemical-free agricultural environment.
Workforce: The farm employs around 15-20 local villagers, providing a source of livelihood for the community. The farm operates using traditional organic farming techniques coupled with innovations in sustainable agriculture.
Land Area: Approximately 20-25 acres of land is used for organic farming, cultivating a range of products based on seasonal crops and local demand.
